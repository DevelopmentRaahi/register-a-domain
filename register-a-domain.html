Thawte SSL Certificates FAQs

A Thawte SSL Certificate is a digital certificate issued by Thawte, a trusted Certificate Authority (CA). It provides secure encryption for online communications and verifies the identity of websites to ensure safe browsing for users.

Thawte offers several types of SSL Certificates, including Domain Validation (DV), Organization Validation (OV), and Extended Validation (EV) certificates. Each type provides a different level of validation and assurance.

Thawte SSL Certificate validation varies by type. DV certificates only require domain ownership verification. OV certificates require both domain ownership and organization verification. EV certificates require a thorough vetting process to ensure the highest level of trust and security

Yes, Thawte SSL Certificates can be used on multiple servers. Thawte also offers wildcard and multi-domain certificates, which allow you to secure multiple subdomains or domains with a single certificate.

The Thawte Trusted Site Seal is a visual indicator displayed on your website to show visitors that your site is secured with a Thawte SSL Certificate. This seal can enhance user trust and confidence in your website's security.

Yes, Thawte SSL Certificates are compatible with all major web browsers and devices. They ensure secure connections across a wide range of platforms, providing seamless user experiences for your website visitors.

Thawte and Sectigo SSL Certificate both offer SSL certificates that provide encryption and authentication for websites. The primary difference lies in their brand reputation and specific offerings. Thawte is known for its strong global recognition and trust, while Sectigo, formerly Comodo, is known for its wide range of affordable and feature-rich SSL certificates.
